Can you create the perfect date before it's too late?



I. THE BEGINNING.

After an exhausting day of work, you head to the local tavern to relax. A beautiful creature approaches you and offers to purchase a drink for you. After blinking away confusion, you realize the creature speaking to you. It's...


The Beast
Aquila the Eagle
Cerberus the Three-Headed Dog

II. GREETINGS.

After agreeing to see each other again soon, you mark the date in your calendar. Soon, the day arrives, and you're wondering what flowers to get for him. You decide on a bouquet of...

White lilies
Red roses
Blue roses
Forget-me-not

III. LOCATION.

The journey there is difficult, and you're sweating by the time you get there. Still, your eyes widen at the meeting place. It completely embodies his personality, at least from what little you know if him. You've arrived at...

A snug cottage deep in the forest
A castle surrounded by greenery and rivers
The heavens, filled with silver clouds and gittering stars
A cozy den tucked safely under the earth

IV. AMBIANCE.

When you arrive, he greets you warmly with a glint in his eyes. He places the flowers in a vase and leads you around his place, all the while with his claws at your back. Despite this gentleman being not-quite-human, you still marvel at his taste in beautiful decor:

Garlands of flowers and herbs on the walls, with candles and cushions on the floor
A spiraling staircase in the middle of the room, with grand paintings and towering bookshelves along the walls
Jars of stardust and the scent of rain in the air
A roaring fireplace with old, cushioned chairs and a plush rug in front of it


You chose correct answers, and incorrect answers.



You're enjoying your date trememdously - the food is rich, the conversation is smooth and natural, and the music fits the mood just right. It's nearly midnight when you both realize how late it is.

"You've come a long way," says the gentleman. "The least I can do is offer you the guest room. I'll take you back home tomorrow."

You accept this generous offer, and find yourself looking out the window at the peaceful night, listening to the night creatures' songs. Your eyes begin to droop...

Your body jolts awake, and you're shrouded in darkness. Something is wrong. The curtains are drawn, and despite the thick covers, there's a chill in the room. Two silvery eyes are glowing in the darkness, the musty smell of unwashed fur staining the air, a blunt claw gently stroking your cheek.

"Why didn't you choose me?" he whispers, his rotten breath washing over your sweating face. "I told you in the beginning. To Date the Wolf, and Not the Others."

"But it's okay. I'll show you what will happen now."

The next morning, you are nowhere near your home, or your gentleman's home, but in the lair of a beast. You don't know where you are, or how long you will stay here, but he prowls around you, with those silvery eyes, wondering how best to savor you.

As time slips by, and you slip in and out of consciousness, the last thing you see is the sliver of a knife. The last thing you feel is the blade slipping across your neck.

BAD END.
